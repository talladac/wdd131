Home

Oman is a fascinating destination that combines the ancient and the modern, the natural and the cultural, in a captivating way. Explore the majestic Jabreen Castle, a masterpiece of Islamic architecture and a symbol of Oman's heritage. Discover the diverse landscapes of Oman, from the rugged mountains and deserts to the pristine coastline and lush oases. Oman is a place where you can sense the soul of Arabia, while enjoying its modern amenities and hospitality.

History

During the 18th century Ahmad ibn Said drove out Persian invaders and became the elected Imam, establishing the dynasty that still rules Oman today.

After signing a treaty of friendship in 1798 (the first between an Arab Gulf state and Britain), Oman and Britain developed increasingly close ties, without the Sultanate ever becoming a colony.

Around the same period the Sultanate also developed relations with France and the Netherlands creating a network of trade and cultural exchanges.

In 1833 Sultan Said of Oman signed a treaty of friendship and commerce with a trade representative of the United States, opening the way to a diplomatic relationship.

About

  • Oman is the oldest independent state in the Arab world
  • It is one of the oldest human-inhabited places on Earth
  • Oman is one of the safest countries in the world
  • It has five UNESCO World Heritage Sites
  • Oman is a bird watcher's paradise
  • The country has a hot, arid climate with high humidity along the coast
  • The official language is Arabic and the currency is the Omani Rial (OMR); third most valuable currency in the world
